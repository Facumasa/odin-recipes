Tiramisu

Follow these steps for an amazing tiramisu

Tiramisu is the epitome of indulgence, a beloved Italian dessert that combines layers of creamy, coffee-infused decadence. This no-bake treat features delicate ladyfinger cookies soaked in bold espresso and often enhanced with a splash of liqueur, like Marsala wine or amaretto. These layers are alternated with a velvety mascarpone cheese mixture, lightly sweetened and whipped to perfection. A dusting of rich cocoa powder on top ties together the dessert's luxurious flavors, creating a balance of bitterness and sweetness that melts in your mouth with every bite.

What makes tiramisu so special is its perfect harmony of textures and flavors. The ladyfingers provide a soft, sponge-like base, absorbing the coffee while maintaining their structure. The mascarpone filling adds a creamy, almost ethereal lightness that contrasts beautifully with the bold espresso and cocoa. Whether served in elegant glasses for individual portions or as a show-stopping layered dish, tiramisu is a timeless dessert that impresses with both its simplicity and sophistication. Perfect for celebrations or as an after-dinner treat, tiramisu is a slice of Italian culinary artistry.

Ingredients

  • Mascarpone cheese
  • 6 large egg yolks
  • 3/4 cups of sugar
  • 1 cup of heavy cream

Steps

  1. Make a liter of hot black coffee
  2. Drink some, you deserve it
  3. Put the rest in a blender
  4. Add all the other ingredients and blend it for 2 min
  5. Put it in a dish/glass
  6. Eat it and watch italians cry
